## Overview

*Placeholder. Introduce the works: the Discourses as transcribed by Arrian from the oral teaching of Epictetus, and the Enchiridion as Arrian's distillation of that teaching into a handbook of practice.*

## The Author

*Placeholder. Epictetus — born a slave in Hierapolis, owned by a freedman of Nero, eventually freed and banished from Rome by Domitian, who founded a school at Nicopolis where he taught until his death. The paradox of a slave who taught freedom.*

## Arrian and the Text

*Placeholder. Lucius Flavius Arrianus — the student who recorded Epictetus's lectures. The relationship between the Discourses and the Enchiridion, and what "original Epictetus" means when nothing is in his own hand.*
